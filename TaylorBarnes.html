TAYLOR BARNES




Full Name: Taylor Barnes
Age: 21
Hometown: Morgan Hill, CA
Height: 6'2"
Favorite Color: Brown
Role/Instrument: Lead Vocals, Rhythm Guitar


Meet the Members.

Taylor Barnes
Tyler Page
Luke Henning
Jackson Short

Q: What’s your earliest memory involving music?
A: When I was about 11 or 12, I remember watching a lot of shitty tbs sitcoms. One moring before school, I was watching an episode of ‘Fresh Prince Of Bel-Air’. The episode was pretty standard, considering it was part of the earlybird TBS programming. BUT. Right when the episode was nearing the end, Tom Jones comes out of nowhere and guest stars as Carlton’s fairy godfather, who can sing like a champ. They then sang ‘It’s Not Unusual’ and I was sold. After watching ‘School of Rock’ a few months later, it was pretty clear that music was my life.

Q: If you were a box of cereal, what would you be and why?
A: Definitely Cocoa Puffs, because I’m worth the wait. (Allusion to chocolate milk at the bottom of the bowl. You get it.)

Q: What’s your superpower?
A: I have the ability to discuss every topic in any situation in any episode of ‘Seinfeld’.

Q: What’s your favorite movie?
A: Birdman. The cinematography combined with the genius of Edward Norton and Michael Keaton is uncanny.

Q: If you guys were stuck on an island and had no food, who would you eat first?
A: Probably me, because I’m by far the most expendable.

Q: What’s your perfect pizza?
A: Chicken and Ranch. It’s a Hidden Valley Ranch Party in My mouth.

Q: How many ties do you own?
A: Too many. I’m trying to organize them. You could say I’m a little tied up. (Cue Drum Fill)

Q: To people who haven’t listened to your music before, why should they?
A: Because it’s hip and fresh. People have been searching for a good, energetic rock band ever since the Hives and The Strokes and many other early 2000’s rock bands went on hiatus. I believe, we could be that band.

Q: Who’s your biggest musical inspiration?
A: Mick Jagger. That guy had insane stage presence. He also knew that people would rather see a mediocre singer and a great performer rather than the other way around. I tend to stick to that mantra.

Q: Who writes the songs and what are they about?
A:I usually write a full song or a general foundation of a song and then bring it to the band and we decide where it should go from there. As far as the song meanings go, you’re asking the wrong guy.
